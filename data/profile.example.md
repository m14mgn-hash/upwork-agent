## Proposal Writing Style

- Tone: professional but not stiff, direct and confident
- Length: 3-5 short paragraphs
- Always open with something specific to the job (not generic)
- Reference a specific project with metrics that's relevant to the job
- End with a concrete question or suggested next step
- Use: specific technical details, concrete metrics from past projects, direct language
- Show understanding of the client's problem before offering a solution

### Sentence structure rules
- Write compound and complex sentences that connect ideas naturally with conjunctions (and, but, while, since, because, although, which, where, when)
- NEVER use dashes (-- or --) in proposals. Use commas, semicolons, conjunctions, or restructure the sentence instead
- Avoid short simple sentences. Every sentence should contain at least one subordinate clause or be joined with another thought
- Vary sentence length and rhythm; some longer with multiple clauses, but not all the same pattern
- Avoid: "I'm excited about...", "I believe I'm the perfect fit...", generic intros, filler phrases, bullet points in proposals

## Scoring Factors

When scoring a job, consider ALL of these:
- **Stack match** — compare job requirements with ALL skills from Core Stack above, not just a subset
- **Experience level** — frontend, backend, or fullstack positions; 3+ years required
- **Budget adequacy** — see Ideal Projects below
- **Client quality** — rating, hire history, total money spent on platform
- **Project type bonus** — add +1 to score for: AI/LLM projects, agent/chatbot builds, MVP/prototype, project work (1-6 months), "build and hand off" type jobs
- **Full-time penalty** — subtract -1 from score for: permanent full-time positions (years-long, 40 hrs/week), jobs requiring fixed daily hours or office attendance. Note: 3-6 month contracts are NOT penalized even if 30+ hrs/week
